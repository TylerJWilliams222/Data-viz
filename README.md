•	A summary of the project.
    The project is heavily inspired by the personal impact of type one diabetes and university life of one of our group members. Type one diabetes often requires the consumption of sugar to keep the indevidual alive and with the unconventual schedual of a uni student sometimes getting that life saving sugar can prove tricky. with students out into the early hours and shops shut finding sugar can be difficult, leaving people to often rely on vending machines. In this project we mapped out the location of the vending machines on campus and added some details about their access. this is not just a graded project as i will continue to keep this webpage up to date and use its data to help myself manage my chronic illness.

In this webpage you will find a Royal Holloway University of London campus map hosted by 'open Street map' with the locations of vending machines on campus marked by clickable interactive pins displaying details of who can access and an image of the building's name plaque for further visu-alisation. 
The ease of access for this information makes having chronic illnesses such as Type one Diabetes considerably easier as keeping control of blood sugar levels sometimes require the consumption of sugary substances quickly. As much as these individuals usually carry their own sugar on their persons, sometimes mistakes happen such as forgetting their sugar or even needing to consume exponentially more than usual, there is a range of reasons why vending machines can become life savers!
